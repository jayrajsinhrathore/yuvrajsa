Kuldevi: Nagnechi Maa

Nagnechi Maa, the Kuldevi of the Rathore clan, holds a significant place in the history and culture of Rajasthan, particularly among the Jodhpur royal family. The origins of her worship trace back to the establishment of the Rathore dynasty in the 13th century, when Rao Jodha, the clan's founder, is said to have sought her blessings for protection and prosperity, leading to the establishment of her temple in the region. Nagnechi Maa is revered as a powerful form of Goddess Durga, symbolizing strength and safeguarding the clan. Her worship is integral to the Rathore identity, with key rituals and festivals often coinciding with Navratri, attracting numerous devotees. The temple has become a pilgrimage site, reinforcing the cultural and spiritual legacy of the Rathore dynasty, as her stories continue to be shared through folk tales and traditional songs, embodying the clan's heritage and devotion across generations.
