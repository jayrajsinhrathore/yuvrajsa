HISTORY

Dhandal Rathore had four children: two sons, Buro and Pabuji, and two daughters, Sonalbai and Pemabai. Upon Dhandal’s death, both sons ruled in their village of Kolu. However, Pabuji was a divine incarnation, born from a celestial nymph, who promised to return to him as a mare when he turned twelve. Tensions escalated when Pabuji received the magical mare Kesar Kalami from the goddess Deval, which Khinchi coveted.

To protect Deval, Pabuji vowed to defend her against Khinchi’s raids. Pabuji overthrew the tyrant Mirza Khan and, after a miraculous rescue by the snake-god Goga, promised his niece Kelam’s hand to him. To secure the marriage, he devised a plan involving a venomous snake bite, leading to a wedding that brought laughter when Pabuji claimed he would plunder she-camels from the demon king Ravana. As he fulfilled his promise, Pabuji defeated Ravana in Lanka and returned with the camels. On the way, he captured the heart of the princess Phulvanti but faced delays due to rivalries over saffron. Eventually, just before his wedding, he was summoned by Deval about Khinchi's theft of her cattle. Pabuji, abandoning the wedding, rode back to confront the Khinchis. His loyal companion Dhebo, known for his selflessness, met a tragic end in the battle against Khinchi’s army. In a climactic showdown, Pabuji spared Khinchi to protect his sister’s honor, leading to further conflict that resulted in the death of his brother Buro. Buro's wife, pregnant and desperate, cut her child, Rupnath, from her body to save him. Raised away from his origins, Rupnath eventually learned the truth and sought revenge. Disguised as a holy man, he infiltrated Khinchi’s stronghold and beheaded him. Afterward, he became a sage, dedicating his life to meditation, ensuring the legacy of the Rathores lived on. Read More

About कुंवर जयराज सिंह राठौड़

कुंवर जयराज सिंह राठौड़, पाबूजी राठौड़ के वंशज हैं। वे कोलू क्षेत्र के युवा राजकुमार हैं। जयराज सिंह का लक्ष्य अपने समुदाय की सेवा करना और अपने पूर्वजों की परंपराओं को आगे बढ़ाना है। वे अपनी शिक्षा और सामाजिक कार्यों में सक्रिय रूप से भाग लेते हैं, जिससे वे नई पीढ़ी के लिए प्रेरणा स्रोत बनते हैं। उनका समर्पण और नेतृत्व गुण उन्हें एक आदर्श बनाते हैं।
